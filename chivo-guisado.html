Chivo Guisado is a classic Latin American stew made with goat meat. These are the instructions for a friendly tomato soup.
you will need:
  • A bunch of tomatoes
  • White onion
  • Garlic
  • Olive oil
  • Vegetable broth
  • You’ll need a medium sized saucepan, or a pot, preferably a pot
Directions:
1. Find inspiration to cook.
In my case, I was listening to a podcast in the middle of the workday hosted by two men, both from the Caribbean and, between impassioned tirades about the potential of the New York Knicks, commisserated on their shared experience as the children of stern, accented immigrants. Children of fathers with feet like cinderblocks and four smiles distributed across the entire latter half of their life. Children of mothers with a love so deep that its incompatible with any kind of distance. He mentions chivo guisado: a beautiful, luxurious dish that takes skill and, more importantly, a sense of purpose. You have a debit card and a trader joe's on your commute home.
2. Cut up the tomatoes into small chunks.
This is performance, we’re hoping that these tomatoes break down in the heat they’ll soon be subjected to, but I’ve never done this really. It may well be that the tomatoes linger long after I expected them to succumb to the warm envelope of olive oil. The tomatoes may defiantly dry themselves out, happy to throw themselves into the abyss if it means that I too, will be left floating in space with no sense of self and no tomato soup. Set these aside
3. Mince garlic and white onion and have some salt and pepper on hand
4. Liberally drizzle a pan with olive oil and set it to medium heat, once a sliver of onion sizzle when added to the pan, add the rest of the onion, garlic, and peppers and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
I’m not sure how long or when this vegetable amalgamation should remain in the pan. I’ve seen my mother do this hundreds, maybe thousands of times, but I haven’t seen my mother cook for several years. Sometimes she calls me and in the long pauses I can hear a faint sizzle that could be the sofrito in a pan behind her. It is more likely an artifact of the phone connection.
I don’t think she cooks too often anymore but I can’t be sure either way, so I listen intently to the crackle.
Is she stirring? How hot is the pan? Did she add chili powder? Why can’t I just ask her? Perhaps that’s the metric, cook the vegetables until you just can't stay on the phone any longer.
5. Add the tomatoes to the pan and cook until tender and easily broken apart.
Keep an eye on these because remember, these tomatoes have more of a backbone than you do. If they persist, smash them as best you can with a wooden spoon. Again, stay focused! You will miss your target often!
6. Add two cups of vegetable broth
If you’re a vegetarian, no changes are necessary. If you’re not a vegetarian and neither are your roommates, still just do it. Who knows who could come over.
7. Let simmer
A recipe like this still doesn’t have to be good. It was not made in good faith and so it won't taste like it was made in good faith. It’s best to prepare at an awkward time: when everyone is scarcely removed from their last meal or else too close to bed.
8:45 PM on a Tuesday night.
8. Eat it if you want. More reasonably: forget it on the stove, clean it up in the morning when no one is home.
